⚔️ Warhammer 40,000

Core Rules Quick Reference

In the grim darkness of the far future, there is only war. This page summarises the essential rules to get your battle started.

🎲 The Golden Rule

Warhammer 40,000 uses six-sided dice (D6). When a rule says "roll a D6", roll one die. When it says "roll 2D6", roll two dice and add them together.

  • Roll equal to or higher than the target number to succeed
  • A roll of 1 always fails, a roll of 6 always succeeds (for most rolls)

🪖 The Turn Sequence

  1. Command Phase — gain Command Points, use Stratagems
  2. Movement Phase — move your units across the battlefield
  3. Shooting Phase — fire ranged weapons at enemy units
  4. Charge Phase — declare and resolve charges
  5. Fight Phase — melee combat, alternating attacks

🏃 Movement

Each unit has a Move (M) characteristic. Models can move up to that distance in inches.

  • Normal Move — move up to M inches
  • Advance — roll a D6 and add to M, but cannot shoot after
  • Fall Back — disengage from melee, cannot shoot or charge

🔫 Shooting

To shoot, pick an eligible unit, select targets, then roll to hit and wound.

  • Hit Roll — roll D6 vs. Ballistic Skill (BS)
  • Wound Roll — compare Strength (S) to Toughness (T)
  • Save Roll — target rolls vs. Armour Save (Sv)
  • Damage — subtract remaining damage from Wounds (W)

⚔️ Melee Combat

In the Fight Phase, units within Engagement Range (1") fight.

  • The player whose turn it is picks first
  • Hit Roll — roll D6 vs. Weapon Skill (WS)
  • Same wound and save process as shooting
  • Units that charged get to fight first
